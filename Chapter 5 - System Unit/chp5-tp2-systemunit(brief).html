System Unit Detailed!

Why are some computers more powerful than others? The answer lies in three words: speed, capacity and flexibility. To understand this, you need to know the functionality of the basic components in the system unit, including the system board, microprocessors, memory, expansion slots and cards, bus lines, ports, cables, and power supply units. The System Unit also knwon as the system chasis, is a container that houses most of the electronic components that make up a computer system. In old generation computers, all system units were present in a separate case. Advances in the miniaturisation of electronic components, however, has led to smaller computers with system units that share containers with other parts of the computer system. As we have previously discussed, a personal computer is the most widely used equipment. It is the most affordable and is designed to be operated directly by an end user. The six most common types of computers are desktop, laptop, tablet, personal digital assistant (PDA), smartphones, and wearable computers. Each has a unique type of system unit.

Desktop

This is the most powerful type of personal computer. Most desktops have thier system unit in a separate case. This case contains the device's electronic components and selected secondary storage devices. Input and output devices, such as monitor, are located outside the system unit. This type of system unit is designed to be placed either horizontally or vertically are sometimes referred to as a tower unit or tower computer, see Figure 5-1. Some desktop computers' manufracturer, like HP, Lenovo and Dell have thier monitor and system unit housed together in the same case. These computers are known as an all-in-one, see Figure 5-2.

Laptops

Laptops are portable, smaller, and as powerful as desktops. These systems units housed with selected secondary storage devices and input devices (keyword and pointing device). Located outside the system unit, the monitor is attached by hinges. There are several specialised laptops with features that make them unique. Some of these are:

‚óè Two-in-one laptops, which include a touch screen and the ability to fold flat like a tablet computer. These laptops offer the advantages of a laptop with the convenience of a tablet, see Figure 5-3.

‚óè Gaming laptops, which include high-end graphics hardware and very fast processors. At one time, most gamers preferred desktop computers. As laptops have become more powerful, gaming laptops have become the favourite primarily due to thier portability.

‚óè Ultrabooks, also known as ultraportables or mini notebooks are lighter and thinner with longer battery life than most laptops. They accomplish these advantages by leaving out components such as optical drives and using energy-efficient microprocessors, see Figure 5-4.

Tablets

Tablets, also known as tablet computers, are the newest and one of the most popular types of computer. They are, effectively, a thin slab with a wide-screen monitor and the system unit located just behind it. Tablets are smaller, lighter, and generally less powerful than laptop computers. Like a laptop, tablets have a flat screen but typically do not have a standard keyboard. Instead, most tablets use a virtual keyboard that appears on the screen and in touch-sensitive. While tablets are very convenient for watching videos or surfing the web, inputting information can be difficult using thier virtual keyboard. Some ways to overcome this limitation are with:

‚óè Keyboard attachments: Several manufracturers are offering keyboard options. For example, HP, Dell, Microsoft, Arteck, and Logitech offer wireless keyboards for tablets.

‚óè Digital pens: These devices assist in selecting options and entering handwritten notes. For example, Microsoft, Samsung, iBall, Huawei and Lenovo offer tablets and digital pens.

‚óè Speech recoginition: This advance feature allows direct verbal communication. For example, both Apple and Google feature excellent speech recoginition features built into iOS and Chrome OS.

Mini tablets are a type of tablets with a smaller screen. While mini tablets operate like a traditional tablet, they typically do not have all the functionality of the bigger tablets. Thier most significant advantage is that they are easier to transport in pockets and purses, see Figure 5-5.

PDA

A Personal Digital Assistant (PDA) is a portable device that functions as a personal information manager. PDAs are used for web browsing, office applications, watching videos, viewing photos or as mobile phones. PDA model feature vary, but current common features include touch screen displays, Bluetooth and Wi-fi connectivity, memory card slots, mobile software applications and multimedia support. PDAs usually include personal information managers for contacts and schedules and always come with software to synchronise desktop or cloud server information.

Smartphones

Smartphones are by far the most popular mobile device. Designed to comfortably fit into the plam of one hand, smartphones have become the indispensable handheld computer. They greatly extend the capabilities of a cell phone by providing computing power. In addition to capturing and sendimg audio and video, smartphones run apps, connect to the Internet, and more. Thier system unit is located behind the display screen.

Wearable Computers

Wearable Computers, also known as wearable devices, are one of the first evolutionary steps to the IoT. These devices contain an embedded computers on a chip that is typically much smaller and less powerful than a smartphones's.
