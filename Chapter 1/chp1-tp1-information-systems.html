● Information Systems

When you think of a personal computer (PC), perhaps you just think of the equipment, such as the screen or the keyboard. Yet, there is more to a personal computer than that. The best way to think about a personal computer is as part of an information system. An information system have serveral parts: people, procedures, software, hardware, data, and the Internet, see Figure 1-1.

  • People: It is easy to overlook people as one of the parts of an information systems. Yet this is what personal computer are all about-making people, end users like you, more productive.
  • Procedures: The rules or guidelines which are to be followed by people when using software, hardware, and data are known as procedures. These procedures are typically documented in manuals written by computer specialists. Software and hardware manufactures provide manuals with thier products. These manuals are provided in printed or electronic form.
  • Software: A program is a step-by-step instruction that tell a computer how to do its work. Software is another name for a program or programs. The purpose of software is to convert data (unprocessed facts) into information (processed facts). For example, a payroll program would instruct the computer to take the number of hours you worked in a month (data) and multiply it by your pay rate (data) to determine how much you are paid for the month (information).
  • Hardware: The equipment that processes the data to create information is called hardware. It includes smartphones, tablets, keyboards, mouse, displays, system units, and other devices. Hardware is controlled by software.
  • Data: The raw, unprocessed facts, including text, numbers, images, sounds, etc. are called data. Processed data yields information. Using the previous example of a payroll program, the data (number of hours worked and pay rate) is processed (multiplied) to yield information (monthly pay).
  • Internet: Almost all information systems connect and interact with other people and computers. THis is done by the Internet. This connectivity greatly expands the capability the usefulness of information systems.
