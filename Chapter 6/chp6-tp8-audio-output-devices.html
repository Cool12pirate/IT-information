Audio-Output Devices

Audio-output devices translate audio information from the computer into sounds that people can understand. The most widely used audio-output devices are speakers and headphones, see Figure 6-25. These devices connect to a sound card within the system unit. This connection can be established by cable to an audio jack on the system unit, or the connection can be wireless. Wireless connections system unit, or the connection can be wireless. Wireless connections typically use Bluetooth techonology. This type of connection require special Bluetooth-enabled speakers and/or headphones. The sound card is used to capture as well as play back recorded sounds. Audio-output devices are used to play music, vocalise translation from one language to another, and communicate information from the computer system to users. Several wireless-wifi devices are Google Assistant, Alexa, microphon, bluetooth, etc.

Combination Input and Output Devices

Many devices combine input and output capabilities. Sometimes this is done to save space. Other times it is done for very specialised applications. Common combination devices include headsets, multifunctional devices, virtual reality head-mounted displays and controllers, drones, and robots.

Headsets

Headsets combine the functionality of microphones and headphones. The microphone accepts audible input, and headphones provide audio output. Headsets are an integral part of serious video games systems, see Figure 6-26.

Multifunctional Devices

Multifunctional Devices (MFD) typically combine the capabilities of a scanner, printer, fax, and copy machine. These multifunctional devices offer a cost and space advantage. They cost about the same as a good printer or copy machine, but require much less space than the single-function devices they replace. Thier disadvantages are lower quality and unreliability. The output quality for any one function is often not quite as good as that of the output quality for any one function is often not quite as good as that of the separate single-purpose devices. The reliability of multifunctional devices suffers because problems with one of the functional parts can make the entire device inoperable. Even so, multifunctional devices are widely used in home and small business offices.

Telephone

Telephones are input and output devices for receiving and sending voice communication. Voice over IP (VoIP) is a set of standard or technologies that support voice and other types of communication over the Internet. Telephony, also known as internet Telephony, is the VoIP application that uses Internet rather than traditional lines to support voice communication. There are many VoIP services available for free that do not require any dedicated hardware. Examples of these services are Skype from Microsoft and Hangouts from Google. Once you subscribe to a service, you can use your smartphone or other type of computer to connect to any other subscriber. These services are free and support video as well as audio; however, both parties must have thier computers on and be logged in to the service to send and receive calls. For an additional fee, Skype allows you to call non-Skype subscribers directly on thier phone.

Drones

Drones were once too expensive for anything but military budget. However, today's drones are inexpensive, faster, and smarter, making them a valuable tool and fun high-tech toy, see Figure 6-28. Drones were most commonly called as RPA (remotely Piloted Aircraft), ROA (Remotely Operated Aircraft), UAS (Unmanned Aircraft System) or FAA (Federal Aviation Administration). But today the common term for drone is UAV i.e. Unamnned Arial Vehical. Drones can fly either in a straight line or around in circles. Most drones take input from a controller from either or a ratio joystick or a Wi-Fi connected tablet or laptop. The drones act as an output device, sending back video and sound to the user. The resulting combination of video and aerial maneuverability has made drones a popular choice for a wide variety of activities ranging from amateur cinematographers to civil engineers. Drone use is on the rise by all types of users. Drones are used in agriculture to apply pesticides and monitor livestock, in disaster relief to look for people in distress, and by the Post Officer to deliver mail. Some other uses of drones are:

Robots

Like drones, robotics has become relatively inexpensive with expanding capabilities. Robots use cameras, microphones, and other sensors as inputs. Based on these inputs, robotic outputs can be as complex as exploring damaged nuclear reactors to as simple as taking a photo. Recent improvements in Robotics software and decrease in hardware prices have made robots more prevalent in industrial and hobbyist workshops. Robots can be found almost everywhere, including in a home vacuuming floors, in a factory assembling cars, on a farm sorting produce, and in hospitals assisting in surgery, see Figure 6-29. Like drones robotics have also done some notable achievements like RoboBee which can cling to the ceiling with static electricity, Tiny Origami Robots which unfolds in stomach and extract information from human body. EMILY is another remote-controlled robot lifeguard which has recently proved its worth by saving hundreds of Syrian refugees from drowning off the Greek island of Lesbos.
