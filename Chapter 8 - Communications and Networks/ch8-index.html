Communications and Network

Why should you read this chapter?

Communications and networks are the backbone of naerly every aspect of modern digital life. In the future, telepresence (the ability to fully experience the reality of a different place without actually being there) will be commonplace. For example, doctors will routinely perform surgery on patients located hailway around the world!
This page covers the things you need to know to be prepared for this ever-changing digital world, including:

  1. Learning about coaxial and fiber-optic cables so you can make smart decisions about home Internet connections.
  2. Using your digital devices in smarter and safer ways by understanding Wi-Fi, satellites, and Bluetooth.
  3. Becoming a digital road warrior using 4G and 5G data networks and GPS.

COMPETENCIES
After reading this page, you should be able to:
1. Explain connectivity, the wireless revolution, and communication systems.
2. Describe physical and wireless communication channels.
3. Differentiate between connection devices and services, including dial-up, DSL, cable, satellite and cellular.
4. Describe data transmission factors, including bandwidths and protocols.
5. Define networks and key network terminology, including network interface cards and network operating systems.
6. Explain different types of networks, including local, home, wireless, personal, metropolitan, and wide area networks.
7. Elaborate upon network architectures, such as, topologies and strategies.
8. Explain the organisational issues related to Internet technologies and network security.
